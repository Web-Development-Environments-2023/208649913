Kareem Abdul-Jabbar



Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is widely considered one of the greatest basketball players of all time.
He's a six-time NBA champion, a two-time NBA Finals MVP, and a 19-time NBA All-Star.
Kareem had the record for the all-time leading scorer with 38,387 points,
a record he held for 38 years and was broken on 2023 by Lebron James.
Off the court, Abdul-Jabbar has been a social activist, advocating for racial equality and education reform.
He's also a prolific author, with several books to his name, and has appeared in movies and television shows.

Fun Facts

  • He was born Ferdinand Lewis Alcindor Jr., but changed his name to Kareem Abdul-Jabbar after converting to Islam in 1971.
  • One of the few NBA players who were honored to recieve a statue outside the team's court.
  • He played college basketball at UCLA and won three consecutive NCAA championships from 1967 to 1969.
  • He is known for his signature "skyhook" shot, which was nearly impossible to block.
  • Kareem appeared in the 1980 comedy movie "Airplane!" and played a co-pilot who is questioned about his basketball skills.
  • Abdul-Jabbar is also an accomplished martial artist and has a black belt in karate.

Q&A

What are the differences between
Web 1.0/ Web 2.0/ Web 3.0?

  • Data and Personalization
  • User Interaction
  • Data ownership and privacy

Where can you see these differences?

  • A Web 1.0 page will be bassicaly a read only page, with user only consuming the data with no personalized data and privacy isn't a concern.
  • A Web 2.0 page will be a more interactive page, with user sharing content in social networks and blogs. Each user will get personalized experience and the privacy of its data is a major concern.
  • A Web 3.0 page will be very interactive giving the user the ability to easily interact with other users and other machines, each user is expected to have very personalized experience generated by AI machines. The page will support high security standards protecting user's privacy.
